Lesson 6: Embeddings and Enrollment Quality

Objective: understand what an embedding is and why enrollment quality gates matter more as headcount grows.

Key ideas

  • An embedding maps a face image to a point in a 128-dimensional space where the same person's faces cluster together. Distance between points is similarity, not identity — a threshold turns it into a decision.
  • Privacy consequence: we store only these numbers. The original image cannot be reconstructed from an SFace vector, and raw crops are discarded immediately after feature extraction.
  • Scale consequence: with 1000 employees, every low-quality enrollment enlarges the false-accept surface for all of them. Gates (one face only, confidence, size, sample consistency) keep the gallery clean at the source.
  • Multiple samples per employee capture pose/lighting variation; matching later takes the best score across samples.

Retrieval question

Why check pairwise similarity between enrollment samples? (Answer: it catches a second person or a corrupted capture sneaking into the sample set before it poisons the stored gallery.)
