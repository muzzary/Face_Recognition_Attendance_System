Lesson 8: Multi-Frame Liveness

Objective: understand why anti-spoofing needs evidence over time, and what each check catches.

Key ideas

  • A single frame cannot distinguish a face from a photo of a face — both produce valid detections and embeddings. Liveness must come from behavior across frames.
  • Check 1, motion presence: live heads always drift slightly. A landmark centroid that stays pixel-still for a whole window means a mounted photo.
  • Check 2, non-rigidity: a hand-held photo moves, but as a rigid plane. Removing translation, scale, and rotation from each frame's landmarks cancels rigid motion; whatever remains is the face itself deforming. Photos leave ~zero residual, live faces do not.
  • Normalizing thresholds by inter-ocular distance makes them independent of camera resolution and standing distance.
  • Honest limitation: replaying a video on a screen produces non-rigid motion and defeats this method. Catching it needs texture/moire analysis or depth sensors — documented, not hidden.

Retrieval question

Why does an incomplete evidence window return UNKNOWN instead of PASSED or FAILED? (Answer: no decision without evidence — logging attendance on a half-window would let a spoof through during the first frames, and failing would bounce every person who just arrived.)
