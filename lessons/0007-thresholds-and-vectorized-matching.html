Lesson 7: Thresholds and Vectorized Matching

Objective: understand threshold trade-offs and why matching stays fast at 1000 employees.

Key ideas

  • A similarity score is not identity. The threshold (0.363 cosine similarity for SFace, from OpenCV's published evaluation) splits scores into match/non-match, trading false accepts (impostor passes) against false rejects (employee bounced).
  • Raising the threshold makes spoofing/misidentification harder but frustrates legitimate users; lowering it does the opposite. Attendance systems usually prefer false rejects — a bounced employee retries, a false accept corrupts payroll data.
  • Scale: instead of looping over employees, all gallery vectors are stacked into one L2-normalized matrix; matching is a single matrix @ probe product. 3000x128 floats is ~0.4 MB and multiplies in microseconds — CPU-friendly at 1000 employees.
  • The clock-in/out toggle plus cooldown turns raw recognitions into meaningful events: standing at the camera for 30 seconds should log once, not thirty times.

Retrieval question

Why does the matcher return the best match's distance even when it is a non-match? (Answer: for observability — logging near-misses lets you tune the threshold from real data instead of guessing.)
